Towards Legally Enforceable Smart Contracts

Abstract

A smart contract is a computer program that is stored and executed on a decentralized system such as a Blockchain. At present, smart contracts have a unique value proposition but cannot be enforced in some of the existing judicial frameworks. In this paper, we propose a framework to create and execute legally binding smart contracts. We experimented with a Distributed Outsourcing De- veloper Marketplace aka Freelancer application use case deployed on Ethereum Blockchain. Our findings are useful in the sense that as per respective national legal frameworks, smart contracts can be made legally enforceable by incorpo- rating crypto primitive like digital signature.
